Profile

Experimental nuclear physicist and Senior Research Scientist (permanent) at RIKEN, with more than fifteen years of front-line research and leadership at international facilities in Japan and Germany (J-PARC, SPring-8, KEK, GSI, DESY). I currently hold leading roles in three international experiments — experiment spokesperson of J-PARC E73 (hypertriton lifetime) and proposal spokesperson of SPring-8 LEPS2 (nucleon short-range correlations) and the J-PARC antideuteron experiment. I made leading contributions to the discovery of the K⁻pp (K̄NN) bound state and to the precise \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\) hypernuclear lifetime measurement, and I am first author of the first observation of a Λπ structure near the K̄N(I=1) threshold at Belle. I have deep, partly original expertise in electromagnetic calorimetry, TES cryogenic microcalorimeters, ultra-high-resolution tracking and streaming data acquisition. I worked at the Institute of Modern Physics (CAS) in 2002–2004 and read for my undergraduate degree at Lanzhou University.

Academic Leadership & Spokesperson Roles

  • J-PARC E73 — hypertriton lifetime — Experiment spokesperson. Beam time completed; \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\) lifetime and \(^{3,4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\) production cross sections published; \(^{3}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\) lifetime analysis ongoing.
  • SPring-8 LEPS2 — nucleon short-range correlations with the Λ hyperon as a probe — Proposal spokesperson (ongoing).
  • J-PARC antideuteron experiment — antideuteron–nucleus interaction and annihilation mechanism — Proposal spokesperson (ongoing).

Signature Achievements

  • Precise measurement of the \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\) hypernuclear lifetime. As spokesperson and corresponding author, proposed the key idea of tagging fast π⁰ with a single high-energy γ and led the measurement, $\tau = 206 \pm 8 \pm 12$ ps (J-PARC E73). Phys. Lett. B 845 (2023); 873 (2026).
  • First observation of a Λπ structure near the K̄N(I=1) threshold. As first author, led the Belle data analysis. Phys. Rev. Lett. 130 (2023).
  • Discovery of the K⁻pp (K̄NN) kaonic nuclear bound state. As a founding member of J-PARC E15, developed the multi-hit TDC system and extracted the signal by partial-wave analysis, giving the first evidence for a deeply bound K⁻pp state. Phys. Lett. B 789 (2019); Phys. Rev. C 102 (2020).
  • First application of TES microcalorimeters to hadronic-atom spectroscopy. Implemented the SDD data analysis and energy calibration at sub-eV precision (J-PARC E62). Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys. 2016, 091D01; Phys. Rev. Lett. 128 (2022).
  • First proposal and demonstration of energy-resolved muon spin spectroscopy (μSR). As first and corresponding author, introduced event-by-event positron-energy selection with lead-glass Cherenkov calorimeters and led the demonstration, a ~30% figure-of-merit gain at no cost in beam time (J-PARC MLF). Nucl. Instrum. Meth. A 1092, 171842, Short communication (2026).
  • Determination of hard two-photon exchange (OLYMPUS) with the PbF₂ luminosity monitor. Led the development of the luminosity monitor, a PbF₂ Cherenkov calorimeter based on Møller/Bhabha scattering, whose precision underpinned the per-mille e⁺p/e⁻p elastic cross-section ratio; OLYMPUS found the hard two-photon contribution significantly weaker than theoretical expectations (DESY). Phys. Rev. Lett. 118 (2017); Nucl. Instrum. Meth. A 826 (2016).
