Research

Hypernuclear Physics (Strange Particles, Literally)

Ever wondered what happens when you throw a hyperon into a nucleus? Me too! My research dives into the weird and wonderful world of hypernuclear physics, where baryons get strange (pun intended). Highlights include:

  • Discovering five new γ-ray transitions from ${}_{\Lambda}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ and ${}_{\Lambda}^{11}\mathrm{B}$ hypernuclei during my Ph.D. (2004-2009). Yes, I was that grad student who actually found something new.
  • Working on the J-PARC E73 experiment (2018-present) to solve said puzzle with world-class precision. (Take that, mysteries of the universe!)

Kaon-related Hadron Physics

Kaons are like the quirky cousins of the particle family. My work explores their interactions and exotic states, revealing the secrets of strangeness in nuclear matter. Key moments include:

  • Confirming the $\mathrm{K}^{-} \mathrm{pp}$ bound state in the J-PARC E15 experiment. (Yes, it exists. You're welcome.)
  • Spotting $\Lambda \pi^{\pm}$ signals near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold at Belle. (Hyperon spectroscopy, anyone?)

Antideuteron Experiment (Antimatter Adventures)

What happens when you create and study antideuterons? (Hint: it’s not your average science experiment.) My research at J-PARC focuses on producing and analyzing antideuterons to probe fundamental symmetries and interactions in the universe. Highlights include:

  • Designing and optimizing experiment for the antideuteron annihilation reaction. (Because antimatter isn’t exactly easy to come by.)
  • Developing advanced detection techniques to identify annihilation products with high precision. (Yes, we’re talking about separating them from a sea of other particles.)
  • Investigating antideuteron annihilation scenario to explore baryon-antibaryon interactions. (Because antimatter deserves its moment in the spotlight.)

Technical Developments (True enjoyment!)

When I’m not unraveling the mysteries of the universe, I tinker with technology to make experiments better. Here are some of my proudest creations:

  • Proposing an energy-sensitive Muon Spin Resonance (muSR) spectrometer that improves precision by 1.5x.
  • Dreaming a waveform analysis method for drift chambers, achieving 40 μm resolution in simulations.
  • Creating a streaming DAQ system for real-time data analysis and leading a Plate Cherenkov detector project for particle identification.
