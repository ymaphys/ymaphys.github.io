Yue Ma

Experimental Nuclear Physicist

Senior Research Scientist (permanent), Few-body Systems Physics Laboratory, RIKEN, Japan

I am an experimental nuclear and particle physicist with more than fifteen years of front-line research and leadership at the J-PARC, SPring-8, KEK, GSI and DESY facilities. I currently serve as experiment spokesperson of J-PARC E73 (hypertriton lifetime) and as proposal spokesperson of the SPring-8 LEPS2 short-range-correlation program and the J-PARC antideuteron experiment. My research uses strangeness — hyperons and kaons — as a probe of the strong interaction and dense nuclear matter. I contributed to the discovery of the K⁻pp (K̄NN) kaonic nuclear bound state and to one of the most precise hypernuclear lifetime measurements (\(^{4}_{\Lambda}\mathrm{H}\)), and I am a member of the Belle / Belle II collaboration.

Research Interests

  • Hypernuclear physics and the neutron-star hyperon puzzle: hyperon–nucleon and three-body (YN / YNN) interactions probed through hypernuclear lifetimes and binding energies, constraining the dense-matter equation of state.
  • Kaon-related hadron physics: antikaon–nucleon dynamics and exotic states such as the K⁻pp bound state and Λ(1405), studied at J-PARC and Belle / Belle II.
  • The Λ hyperon as a probe of short-range correlations: a novel approach to the EMC effect and nucleon–nucleon short-range pairs, en route from SPring-8 LEPS2 toward an electron–ion collider.
  • Antideuteron–nucleus interactions: the first dedicated study of antideuteron annihilation and matter–antimatter dynamics at J-PARC and Belle II.

A note: phase-space normalization

A short utility I use to convert the relative weights returned by ROOT's TGenPhaseSpace into absolute phase-space integral weights. The normalization factor for an n-body decay of a parent of mass M with available energy E is:

\[ \text{factor} = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } n < 2 \text{ or } E \leq 0 \text{ or } M \leq 0 \\ \dfrac{2 \pi^{n} (2E)^{n-2}}{(2\pi)^{3(n-1)} M (n-2)!} & \text{if } n \geq 2 \end{cases} \] 

// Calculate the phase-space normalization factor
Double_t CalculatePhaseSpaceFactor(Int_t numParticles, Double_t availableEnergy, Double_t parentMass)
{
    if (numParticles < 2 || availableEnergy <= 0.0 || parentMass <= 0.0)
    {
        std::cerr << "Error: invalid parameters for phase-space calculation" << std::endl;
        return 0.0;
    }

    double piPower       = numParticles;     // pi factor for each particle
    double twoPiCubePower = numParticles - 1; // (2pi)^3 divisor for each particle
    double teCmTmPower   = numParticles - 2;  // available-energy factor for intermediate states

    double factor = 2 * pow(TMath::Pi(), piPower) /
                    pow(2.0 * TMath::Pi(), 3 * twoPiCubePower) *
                    pow(2.0 * availableEnergy, teCmTmPower) /
                    parentMass;

    if (numParticles > 2)
    {
        factor /= double(factorial(numParticles - 2));
    }

    return factor;
}
