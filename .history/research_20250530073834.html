Research

Hypernuclear Physics (Strange Particles, Literally)

Ever wondered what happens when you replace a neutron or proton with hyperon in a nucleus? My research dives into the weird and wonderful world of hypernuclear physics, where baryons get strange (pun intended). Current highlights include:

  • J-PARC E73 Hypertriton Lifetime Puzzle: Aiming to solve the mysterious hypertriton lifetime puzzle using the innovative $^3\mathrm{He}(\mathrm{K}^-, \pi^0)$ $_\Lambda^3\mathrm{H}$ reaction. We're using a PbF₂ calorimeter to catch those sneaky high-energy gamma rays and nail down the lifetime with unprecedented precision. (Take that, heavy-ion experiments!)
  • LEPS2 Short-Range Correlations: Pioneering a novel approach to probe nuclear short-range correlations using Λ hyperons as our secret weapon. By exploiting the Λ's isospin-zero nature in the exclusive reaction $^Z\mathrm{A}(\gamma, \mathrm{K}^0 + p + \Lambda)^{Z-1}(\mathrm{A}-2)$, we're isolating SRC contributions like never before. (Because who needs conventional methods anyway?)
  • Past discoveries include five new γ-ray transitions from ${}_{\Lambda}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ and ${}_{\Lambda}^{11}\mathrm{B}$ hypernuclei during my Ph.D. (2004-2009). Yes, I was that grad student who actually found something new.

Kaon-related Hadron Physics

Kaons are like the quirky cousins of the particle family. My work explores their interactions and exotic states, revealing the secrets of strangeness in nuclear matter. Key moments include:

  • Confirming the $\mathrm{K}^{-} \mathrm{pp}$ bound state in the J-PARC E15 experiment. (Yes, it exists. You're welcome.)
  • Spotting $\Lambda \pi^{\pm}$ signals near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold at Belle. (Hyperon spectroscopy, anyone?)

Antideuteron-Nucleus Interactions (Antimatter Adventures)

What happens when antimatter meets ordinary nuclei? My research at J-PARC's K1.8 beam line is designed to find out! This cutting-edge experiment explores antideuteron-nucleus interactions to unlock the secrets of the strong nuclear force in antimatter systems. Current objectives include:

  • Optical Potential Determination: Mapping out the antideuteron-nucleus optical potential using advanced detection methods and the GiBUU transport model. (Because understanding antimatter interactions isn't exactly straightforward.)
  • Annihilation Mechanisms: Investigating how antideuterons annihilate by measuring partial to coherent annihilation cross-section ratios and analyzing pion momentum distributions. (Yes, we're literally watching antimatter disappear.)
  • Future Antimatter Studies: Opening new doors for antimatter research by deepening our understanding of antideuteron properties and antinucleon physics. (Because antimatter deserves its moment in the spotlight.)

Technical Developments (True enjoyment!)

When I’m not unraveling the mysteries of the universe, I tinker with technology to make experiments better. Here are some of my proudest creations:

  • Proposing an energy-sensitive Muon Spin Resonance (muSR) spectrometer that improves precision by 1.5x.
  • Dreaming a waveform analysis method for drift chambers, achieving 40 μm resolution in simulations.
  • Creating a streaming DAQ system for real-time data analysis and leading a Plate Cherenkov detector project for particle identification.
